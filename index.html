About

I’m Dr. Chen Hajaj, a faculty member in the Industrial Engineering and Management Department at Ariel University. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Bar-Ilan University under the mentorship of Professors David Sarne and Avinatan Hassidim. Afterward, I completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Vanderbilt University with Professor Yevgeniy Vorobeychik, where I also served as a Data Science Fellow at the Vanderbilt Data Science Institute.

I combine advanced machine learning with strategic analysis in medical data science, traffic classification, adversarial AI, and incentive design to enhance patient outcomes, secure networks, defend against malicious attacks, and foster fair collaboration.

• Network Analysis: Enhancing encrypted traffic classification to improve Quality of Service (QoS) and Quality of Experience (QoE), while uncovering hidden patterns that guide effective security strategies.

• Human-Computer Interaction: Developing personalized recommendation systems that interpret user preferences across multiple modalities, enabling highly engaging and customized experiences.

• Medical Data Science: Applying machine learning to analyze medical records, predict patient outcomes and potential deteriorations, and personalize treatment plans for improved patient care.

• Responsible AI Development: Creating ethical, adaptable algorithms designed to withstand adversarial manipulation in critical technology contexts.

Interested in cutting-edge research and eager to make a real impact? I'm looking for motivated M.Sc. and Ph.D. students to join my lab. Together, we’ll tackle challenging problems, publish influential findings, and drive meaningful innovation in a supportive, collaborative environment. If you share my passion for pushing the boundaries of technology and research, I invite you to reach out and explore the opportunities we can create—together.
